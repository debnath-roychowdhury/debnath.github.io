Mutton Biryani

  • Time: 1hr 45 mins
  • Serves: 2 persons
Ingredients Quantity
Basmati rice 500gm
Bay leaves 2
Black cardamom 2
Black peppercorn 6
Green cardamom 6
Cinnamon sticks 2
Cloves 6
Salt 3 tsp
Mutton 1 kg
Ginger paste 1 tsp
Garlic paste 1 tsp
Curd 4 tsp
Coriander powder 1 tsp
Cumin powder 1tsp
Chilli powder 1 tsp
Onion Slice 4 slice
Green chilli 4 pieces
Mastered Oil 1 cup
Ghee 1 tsp

Step 1: Marinate the mutton in advance (2 hours before).
Step 2: Prepare fried onions or Barista.
Step 3: Take the fried onions out with a slotted spoon or ladle and keep them on a paper-towel lined plate.
Step 4: Heat ghee in a thick-bottomed pan.
Step 5: Add remaining sliced onions and green chillies. Cook, stirring continuously, till onions are light golden brown.
Step 6: Add ginger paste and garlic paste and mix well.
Step 7: Add marinated mutton and cook on high heat for seven to eight minutes.
Step 8: Add coriander powder, cumin powder and red chilli powder. Mix thoroughly.
Step 9: Stir in three cups of water, bring it to a boil, reduce heat and cook covered till mutton is almost cooked.
Step 10: Add tomatoes, salt, garam masala powder and fresh coriander leaves.
Step 11: Cook for 15 minutes on medium heat, stirring occasionally. The ghee would be separated from the spices and there should not be any watery gravy to the meat.
Step 12: Prepare the rice
1.Use only good quality long grain Basmati rice.
2. Soak the rice for 20 minutes in water.
3. Wash well till the water runs clear.
4. Drain all the water.
Step 13 In a small piece of cloth take cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, javitri, jaiphal, black peppercorn, shahi jeera, star anise and tie a knot to make a bag (potli).
Step 14 Bring 750 ml water to boil, add rice, bay leaf, salt and potli, cover and cook till rice is done 1/3rd.
Step 15 Drain the water & remove the whole masala potli.
Step 16: Prepare the saffron-milk
1.Take 1/4th cup warm milk in a cup and dissolve saffron strands in the milk.
2.Cover and wait for 20 minutes.
3. Add rose water and kewra essence in the milk.
4. Mix well and cover.
5. Keep aside.
Step-17: Layer the biryani
1.Take a large heavy bottom pan with tight fitting lid.
2.Add 2tbsp ghee to the pan. Melt the ghee on low heat. Turn and rotate the pan carefully so the ghee can coat the bottom and sides of the pan. Switch off the heat.
3.Add a layer of cooked rice, then cooked meat pieces, sprinkle saffron water, add fried onion slices and ghee.
4.Again add a layer of rice, then meat...go on like this till you are done. Top and bottom layer will be of rice.
5.Cover with chopped pudina and coriander, fried onion and slit green chillies and juice of half a lemon.
6.Put the lid on. Seal the pan with flour dough or aluminium foil, then put the lid. Keep the heat to lowest. And cook the Biryani in this 'Dum' process for 40 minutes.
7.Make sure your pan is heavy-bottomed or the rice will burn. Or you can place a flat tawa then keep the pan on that tawa.
8.After 40 minutes switch off the heat and let the biryani stand for another 10 minutes. Transfer to a serving bowl.
Step-18 Serve with raita and salad.

Chicken Momo

  • Time: 45 mins
  • Serves:2 persons
Ingredients Quantity
Flour 450 gm
Refined Oil 1 tsp
Salt 1 tsp
Minced Chicken 250 gm
Ginger paste 1 tsp
Chilli Garlic paste 1 tsp
Coriander leaves 1/2 cup
Red bell pepper 1/2 cup
Onion 1 piece
Green chilli 4 pieces
Water 1/2 litre

Step 1: Chop the vegetables.
Step 2: Boil the minced chicken.
Step 3: Prepare the dough and filling for Chicken Momos.
Step 4: Flatten the dough balls and fill it with chicken stuffing, and steam for 20 minutes.
